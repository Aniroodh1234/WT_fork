The Apple Story

Innovation that changed the world

1976

The Beginning

Apple Computer Company was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in a garage in Los Altos, California. The Apple I was sold as a motherboard with CPU, RAM, and basic textual-video chips.

1977

Apple II

The Apple II revolutionized the computer industry with the introduction of the first-ever color graphics. It became one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers.

1984

Macintosh Arrives

Apple launched the Macintosh with its famous "1984" Super Bowl commercial. It was the first mass-market personal computer featuring a graphical user interface and a mouse.

1998

iMac Revolution

Steve Jobs returned to Apple and introduced the iMac G3, featuring a translucent plastic design that came in various colors. It marked the beginning of Apple's design revolution.

2001

iPod & iTunes

Apple released the iPod, changing the way people listen to music forever. "1,000 songs in your pocket" became reality, and iTunes Store followed in 2003.

2007

iPhone Launch

Steve Jobs introduced the iPhone, combining a phone, iPod, and internet communicator. It revolutionized the smartphone industry and changed how we communicate.

2010

iPad Era

Apple unveiled the iPad, creating an entirely new category of devices. It bridged the gap between smartphones and laptops, becoming a powerful tool for creativity and productivity.

2015

Apple Watch

Apple entered the wearable technology market with the Apple Watch, the company's most personal device yet, focusing on health, fitness, and communication.

2020

Apple Silicon

Apple introduced the M1 chip, marking the transition from Intel processors to custom Apple silicon. This brought unprecedented performance and efficiency to Mac computers.

2023

Vision Pro

Apple announced Vision Pro, a revolutionary spatial computer that blends digital content with the physical world, opening new possibilities for work, entertainment, and connection.
